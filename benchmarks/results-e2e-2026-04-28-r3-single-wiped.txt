KEY OBSERVATIONS
================
1. Zero Azure traffic. Peer transport correctly serves 100% of chunks.
2. Single-pod cold-read time (584s) is nearly identical to the per-pod
   PASS1 time when ALL 17 pods peer-fetch concurrently in run #2 (mean
   551s, max 740s). The peer-fetch transport scales: contention from
   16 concurrent peers fetching from ~1/16 of the cluster does not
   meaningfully degrade per-pod read latency.
3. Cache-resident PASS2 (217s) matches run #2's mean (176s) within
   normal variance. The wiped pod's cache fill matches its peers
   exactly: 6278 inserts vs ~6289 across other pods (the 11-chunk
   delta is the empty-content_length zero-byte files filtered out
   during run #2's hydrate but NOT during pass1 reads).
